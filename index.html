Your Italian Road Trip: Bergamo to Saas-Fee

Detailed Daily Agenda

Monday: Discover Bergamo & Travel to Verona
  • 09:00 Start Exploring Bergamo: Focus on Città Alta (Upper Town). Take the funicular or walk up.
  • 09:15 Discover Città Alta: Wander Piazza Vecchia, see the Basilica di Santa Maria Maggiore & Cappella Colleoni, walk along the Venetian Walls (UNESCO).
  • 12:00 Lunch in Bergamo Città Alta (approx. 1hr).
  • 13:00 Depart from Bergamo Center.
  • ~14:20 Arrive in Verona: Check into B&B, leave luggage, find parking.
  • 15:00 Explore Verona: Start with Piazza Bra, see the Arena exterior, perhaps walk towards Piazza delle Erbe.

  • Evening Relax or evening stroll. Overnight in Verona.
🏛️ Culture & Sights (Bergamo - Morning)
  • Città Alta (Upper Town): The historic heart, accessible by funicular or foot.
  • Piazza Vecchia: Main square with Palazzo della Ragione and Torre Civica (Campanone).
  • Basilica di Santa Maria Maggiore & Cappella Colleoni: Stunning adjacent religious buildings with intricate interiors.
  • Venetian Walls (UNESCO): Walk along sections of the massive 16th-century fortifications for great views.
  • Funicular: A fun way to get up to Città Alta.

🏛️ Culture & Sights (Verona - Afternoon/Next Morning)
  • Piazza Bra: Verona's main square, perfect for soaking in the atmosphere. Site of the Arena.
  • Arena di Verona: Impressive Roman amphitheater. Consider booking an interior visit or even an opera performance if available/interested.
  • Casa di Giulietta (Juliet's House): Visit the famous courtyard and balcony (expect crowds, entrance fee for house museum).
  • Piazza delle Erbe: Historic market square with beautiful frescoes on surrounding buildings and the Torre dei Lamberti.
  • Ponte Pietra: Roman arch bridge offering lovely views of the Adige River and Castel San Pietro hill.
  • Castel San Pietro: Walk or take the funicular up for panoramic views of Verona (especially beautiful at sunset).

Tuesday: Discover Verona & Travel to Padova
  • 09:00 Continue Exploring Verona: Visit key sites like Arena interior, Juliet's House, Piazza delle Erbe, Ponte Pietra, Castel San Pietro viewpoint.
  • 13:00 Lunch in Verona (approx. 1hr).

🏛️ Culture & Sights (Padova - Afternoon/Next Morning)
  • Cappella degli Scrovegni: Absolute must-see for Giotto's frescoes. Booking online far in advance is ESSENTIAL.
  • Prato della Valle: Immense elliptical square with a central island surrounded by statues. Great for a walk.
  • Basilica di Sant'Antonio (Il Santo): Grand pilgrimage church holding the tomb of St. Anthony.
  • Palazzo della Ragione: Medieval town hall with a vast frescoed hall, located between Piazza delle Erbe and Piazza della Frutta (historic market squares).
  • Orto Botanico: Padua's botanical garden is a UNESCO site, one of the oldest in the world.
  • Caffè Pedrocchi: Historic café, worth seeing even just from the outside or for a quick coffee.

🏛️ Culture & Sights (Vicenza - Brief Stop)
  • Piazza dei Signori: The heart of Vicenza, dominated by Palladio's Basilica.
  • Basilica Palladiana: Andrea Palladio's stunning loggia wrapped around the medieval Palazzo della Ragione. Admire the architecture.
  • Teatro Olimpico: Palladio's final masterpiece, a Renaissance theatre with an incredible fixed stage set (allow ~1 hour if visiting inside).
  • Corso Palladio: Stroll along the main street lined with elegant palaces, many by Palladio.

🏛️ Culture & Sights (Brescia - Afternoon/Next Morning)
  • Piazza della Loggia: Beautiful Renaissance square with the Palazzo della Loggia (town hall) and the fascinating astronomical clock tower.
  • Piazza Paolo VI (Piazza Duomo): See the contrast between the round, Romanesque Duomo Vecchio (Old Cathedral) and the larger, Baroque/Neoclassical Duomo Nuovo (New Cathedral).
  • Roman Brescia (UNESCO Site): Explore the Capitolium (Roman temple) and the nearby Roman Theatre ruins. Impressive remains.
  • Museo di Santa Giulia (UNESCO Site): Outstanding city museum housed in a vast monastic complex. Covers history from prehistory to the Venetian age. Allow significant time (2-4 hours) if you plan a full visit.
  • Castello di Brescia: Fortress on Cidneo Hill overlooking the city. Offers great views, houses museums (Arms Museum, Risorgimento Museum), and has pleasant grounds for a walk.

🏛️ Culture & Sights (Arona - Evening/Next Morning)

  • Rocca Borromea di Arona: Public park containing the ruins of a Borromeo castle, offering fantastic panoramic views over the southern end of Lake Maggiore.
  • Statua di San Carlo Borromeo (Sancarlone): Short drive/walk outside town. Enormous 17th-century hollow copper statue you can climb inside for views.
  • Arona Town Center: Explore the streets around Piazza del Popolo.

Friday (Option 1): Arona to Saas-Fee via Simplon Pass
  • 09:00 Enjoy Arona: Final coffee by the lake, maybe visit the Rocca.
  • 10:30 Depart from Arona. Check Simplon Pass road status AND car train status before leaving!
  • 10:30 Drive & Explore towards Saas-Fee: (~2 hr 20 min driving + stops). Route: A26 -> SS33 -> Domodossola -> Simplon Pass/Train -> Brig -> Visp -> Saas-Fee.
    • Potential Stops: Lake Orta detour (allow 1.5-2hr extra), Domodossola (brief), Simplon Pass viewpoint.
    • Simplon Car Train (Autoverlad): Operates Brig (CH) - Iselle di Trasquera (IT), duration ~20 mins. Runs frequently during the day (approx. every 90 mins, e.g., 06:00 - 22:00, but check current official SBB/BLS timetable as schedules vary seasonally and due to potential track work). Useful if pass road conditions are poor or for variety. Tickets usually bought before boarding.
  • ~15:30 Arrive Saas-Fee Parking: Park car, transfer to accommodation (electro-taxi/walk/shuttle).
  • 16:00 Explore Saas-Fee Village: Settle in, walk around the car-free village.
  • 19:00 Dinner in Saas-Fee (approx. 1hr).
  • Evening Relax. Overnight in Saas-Fee.

Highlighting the Differences (Thursday/Friday)

  • Option 1 (Arona Overnight):
    • (+) Breaks up the driving, more relaxed pace on Thursday and Friday.
    • (+) Allows dedicated time to experience the charm of Lake Maggiore in Arona.
    • (+) Shorter driving distances each day compared to Option 2's Thursday.
    • (-) Less time available for exploring Brescia on Thursday morning.
    • (-) Arrive in Saas-Fee later on Friday, meaning less time there on arrival day.
  • Option 2 (Direct to Saas-Fee):
    • (+) Maximizes time in Saas-Fee with a full day for exploration/activities on Friday.
    • (+) Allows slightly more time in Brescia on Thursday morning.
    • (-) Involves a significantly longer driving day on Thursday (~4 hours + stops).
    • (-) Skips the overnight experience and focused exploration of Lake Maggiore/Arona.
    • (-) Might feel more tiring due to the long drive after sightseeing.
